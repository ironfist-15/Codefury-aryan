Information About Natural Disasters

Natural disasters are extreme, sudden events caused by environmental factors that result in significant damage or disruption. Below, you'll find information on various types of natural disasters and tips on how to stay safe during them.

Earthquakes

Earthquakes occur due to the sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust, resulting in seismic waves. They can cause buildings to collapse, landslides, and tsunamis.

  • How to Stay Safe: Drop to your hands and knees, cover your head and neck, and stay away from windows and heavy objects.

Learn more about Earthquakes

Floods

Floods occur when water overflows onto normally dry land, often due to heavy rainfall or rapid snowmelt. They can lead to property damage and loss of life.

  • How to Stay Safe: Move to higher ground, avoid driving through flooded areas, and stay informed about flood alerts.

Learn more about Floods

Landslides

Landslides occur when soil, rock, and debris move down a slope due to gravity, often triggered by heavy rainfall, earthquakes, or volcanic activity. They can bury homes and roads under debris.

  • How to Stay Safe: Evacuate if advised, secure your home, and prepare an emergency kit with essential supplies.

Learn more about Hurricanes

Hurricanes

Hurricanes are large, powerful storms that form over warm ocean waters and can cause severe wind damage, heavy rainfall, and flooding.

  • How to Stay Safe: Evacuate if advised, secure your home, and prepare an emergency kit with essential supplies.
